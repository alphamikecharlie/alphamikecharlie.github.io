About

Portfolio

Robot Learning for Search and Rescue

In this project, I am investigating the use of reinforcement learning to train a robot to perform a search and rescue operation in the presence of a threat actor. The goal is to develop an agent that can locate and rescue survivors while avoiding detection and capture by the threat actor. I am exploring different reward and state-space structures to optimize the agent's performance in this complex and dynamic environment. The project has implications for disaster response and emergency management, where quick and effective search and rescue operations can save lives.

Teaching Value Alignment in Decision-Making to Robots

This project aims to align autonomous agents' decision-making with human values by training them with ethical exemplars. We propose an architecture that uses Inverse Reinforcement Learning to teach an agent different ethical behaviors from human demonstrations. Our tests on a home invasion scenario show convergence and successful execution of the policies, both virtually and physically, demonstrating the potential of our architecture as a template for other ethically-charged scenarios.

Deceptive Robots for Game-Playing and Pill Sorting

In this project, I am investigating the use of deceptive robots in scenarios such as game-playing with children and pill sorting. The aim is to explore how robots can be designed and programmed to deceive humans in a way that can be beneficial in certain contexts.
